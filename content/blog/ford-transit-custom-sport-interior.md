### Cargo Space

The Ford Transit Custom Sport is designed with optimal load capacity in mind. From the get-go in 2012, the short-wheelbase (L1) model set a benchmark by being the only vehicle in its class capable of accommodating three Euro pallets loaded to a height of 1.0 meter. Today, it offers a cargo space ranging from 6.0 cubic meters in the L1 H1 model to 8.3 cubic meters in the L2 H2 configuration. This space is enhanced by a full bulkhead and the capability to load three Europallets directly on the floor. The practicality extends with the inclusion of a large sliding door on the passenger side and an optional second sliding door, ensuring ease of loading and unloading. The rear doors open wide, and the load area is well-shaped with minimal intrusions, which includes a metal bulkhead with a load-through hatch for elongated items like pipes and planks.

### Ease of Cleaning Ford Transit Custom Sport Interior

Maintaining the cleanliness of a commercial vehicle is crucial for operational efficiency and professionalism. The Ford Transit Custom Sport addresses this need with a tough vinyl load floor liner, standard on Trend, Limited, and Sport models. This highly durable surface is easy to clean, helping to keep the vehicle in top condition after the workday. Additionally, the flat load floor and near-vertical walls simplify the cleaning process, allowing for quick and thorough upkeep.

### Ergonomics Ford Transit Custom Sport Interior

Ergonomics play a significant role in the design of the Ford Transit Custom Sport, ensuring that drivers and passengers experience comfort and ease during use. The vehicle features a new flat cab floor design, which not only allows for convenient walk-through access but also provides more room to maneuver. The redesigned seats offer better comfort, and the driver’s seat in the Base model includes eight-way adjustments along with reach and rake adjustments for the steering wheel. However, some ergonomic challenges remain, such as the placement of heating and ventilation controls, which are less accessible due to their position on the far side of the gear lever pod. Despite these minor setbacks, the overall ergonomic design has been enhanced significantly, especially with the introduction of a ‘floating’ touchscreen infotainment system in higher-specification models and improved in-cab storage solutions post the 2018 facelift.

The Ford Transit Custom Sport’s focus on practicality and usability makes it a top contender in its class, providing a comfortable, efficient, and versatile vehicle solution for a wide range of users.

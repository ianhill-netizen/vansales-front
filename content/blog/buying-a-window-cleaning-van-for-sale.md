## **What is a Window Cleaning Van?**

The name “window cleaning van” is both very descriptive and somewhat vague at the same time. It’s a van for window cleaning, sure. But what kind of windows? Does it clean its own windows or those of other cars? Or maybe house windows?

The textbook definition of a window cleaning van is a van that is used by a window cleaning business to assist in their window cleaning tasks. For example, a business providing window cleaning services will move around with a van that contains its supplies and equipment. Since the van caters exclusively to help with window cleaning, it becomes…a window cleaning van.

### **2.  Water tanks**

Another distinct feature of window cleaning vans is that they usually have a water tank installed in the back. The purpose of the tank is to carry a large amount of water for cleaning purposes.

The exact type and size of water tank that you get depends on the van that you purchase. However, the usual capacity for these tank(s) is a few hundred liters.

### **3.  Water hoses, hose reels, and pressured pumping system**

The presence of the water tanks alone isn’t very helpful. Cleaning vans also typically come with a pressured pumping system that allows the water in the tanks to come out at a high speed. This makes the water ideal to use for cleaning purposes.

To carry the water, these vans are also equipped with hoses and hose reels. In some cases, the hoses may have to be separately purchased and installed. The purpose of the hose “reel” is to let you store the hose easily by coiling it up, and then taking it out only as much as required.

### **4.  Holders and brackets on the inside**

Another quality of window cleaning vans is that they usually have holders and brackets to store items like wipers, mops, squeegees, and the like. They are typically installed along the inner walls of the van so that long-handled cleaning equipment (like the ones we just enumerated) can be stored without taking up a lot of space.

The exact number or style of these holders and brackets depends on the van that you buy. If your business is large and your daily window cleaning jobs are numerous, you should look for a window cleaning van for sale that comes with a sufficient amount of holding space for your equipment. But, we will get more into this in the penultimate section of this post.

### **5.  Integrated ladders and/or ladder brackets on the outside**

Window cleaning jobs can be very diverse. As we mentioned earlier, sometimes they can mean cleaning the windows of a small establishment, whereas, at times, they can mean cleaning the windows of a multi-story building.

In consideration of the latter type of jobs, window cleaning vans are also usually equipped with one of two things:

-   Integrated ladders
-   Ladder brackets

When we say “integrated ladders” here, we mean ladders that are attached to the roof of the van and can be opened and closed when needed.

On the other hand, ladder brackets refer to simple holding spaces that are made on the outside of the van to easily hold ladders. Telescopic ladders are very commonly used by companies that provide cleaning services. These types of ladders can be shrunk down to% of their size and carried around with ease. That is what the brackets are for.

### **6.  Large back storage area**

This is yet another quality that distinguishes window cleaning vans from other normal ones.

Other than water tanks and wipers, etc., there are many other pieces of equipment that you may have to store in your van. For example, cleaning supplies like cleaning clothes, buckets, etc., also have to be carried around by window cleaners.

To cater to the need to store these miscellaneous items, window cleaning vans usually come with a spacious back area.

### **7\.   Interior lighting in the back area**

When it comes to normal storage vans or even passenger vans, having a light in the back area is not essential. It’s helpful, yes, but not essential. The reason for that is simple. Normal storage vans and passenger vans aren’t used to keep very small and intricate items and objects in them. Storage vans typically transport stuff in boxes, suitcases, and so on.

However, when it comes to window cleaning vans, there necessarily has to be a proper lighting arrangement in the back. This is because, in the back area of these vans, there are numerous small items and pieces of equipment. Not only is it difficult to get them out but it’s also dangerous. If you handle heavy equipment in the back of the van with no light, you can hurt yourself.

## **How to Pick the Best Window Cleaning Van for Your Business?**

Now that we’ve done looking at the distinct features of a window cleaning van, let’s move on to discuss how you can pick the best one for yourself.

### **1\.   Step one: Check whether it has all the features and options you need**

In the section above, we’ve looked at all the different components and features that a cleaning van should have. When you’re buying one for yourself, you should carefully check whether the contemplated van has all the features you need or not.

For example, if your business is not very large at the time, you may not want a very big van with lots of holders and brackets, etc. You can stick with a simple van that has a simple back storage area.

On the other hand, if your business is booming and you have a lot of daily jobs cleaning big buildings, then you should look for a van that comes with a large water tank, a ladder holder and everything.

### **2\.   Step two: Check the price and the budget**

After the features comes the price. Depending on the size of the van, the engine, as well as the features, the prices of window cleaning vans can vary quite a lot. Make sure that the van you’re purchasing is in your budget.

Also, make sure that the van you’re looking at actually fits the price. You can take evaluations from different dealers for this, and you can check the price of the same van at other places.

### **3.  Step three: If you’re buying a used van, have it properly inspected**

Depending on what your budget is, you could be looking to buy a used van instead of a brand-new one. If that is the case, you should carefully check the condition and upkeep of the contemplated vehicle before finalizing the purchase.

You can get the assistance of a professional for this purpose. Having a proper inspection can help you learn about all the defects of the van, even the ones that aren’t readily visible.

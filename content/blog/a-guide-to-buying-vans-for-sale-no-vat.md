## **What is VAT?**

VAT stands for Value Added Tax. It is a government tax that is levied on the sale of a product at every stage and step of the supply chain. In other words, when a manufacturer sells a product to a retailer, the retailer pays the VAT on it. Then, when the retailer sells the product to the consumer, the latter pays the VAT on it.

When businesses and retailers sell items to their consumers, they make back the amount that they have paid as VAT. If the business has paid more VAT than the amount they get from the customer, they can claim the excess amount from the tax authorities.

The reason for explaining this all is that while everyone in the supply chain has to pay the VAT, it only really negatively affects the final consumer.

## **What is VAT charged on?**

The VAT tax is charged on all types of goods and even services. Most of the things that you buy in grocery stores are subject to VAT. Furniture, utensils, electronics, etc., are all good examples.

The same goes for vehicles like cars and vans. When they are first purchased, you have to pay the VAT on them. But, there are some ways—legal ones, keep in mind—in which you can buy a van without paying the tax on it.

## **What is the meaning of buying a van with no VAT?**

The important thing to keep in mind about buying vans without VAT is that it doesn’t mean that the VAT is not paid on them at all.

Rather, it simply means that the tax is paid by someone else, like a previous owner, and you don’t have to pay it again.

Considering the above, you’ll understand that only used or second-hand vans are the ones that you can buy without worrying about VAT.

Here’s how it all works..  First of all, when the car retailer buys a car from the manufacturer, the former pays the VAT tax on it. However, since the car retailer is a VAT-registered business, it gets to charge the same tax to the customers when the car is sold again..  If the customer buying the car is not a VAT-registered entity, or is just an individual, it means that they have to pay the VAT on the initial purchase, but they can’t charge it again when reselling the car.

What you want to look for is a second-hand car that is either being sold by another person or by a business that doesn’t claim VAT.

### Design and Practicality

The Defender Hard Top’s design strikes a balance between the iconic Defender look and modern practicality. On the exterior, it closely resembles the passenger models, with the notable exception of solid panels replacing the rear side windows. This gives the vehicle a classic Defender appearance, especially when paired with a contrasting white roof finish.

Inside, the vehicle has been thoughtfully converted for commercial use by Land Rover Special Vehicle Operations (SVO). The rear seats have been removed and replaced with a tough, flat load floor that extends from the side-opening tailgate to the full-height bulkhead behind the front seats. This design maximizes cargo space while maintaining the vehicle’s structural integrity and driving refinement.

The Defender Hard Top’s cargo area includes several practical features:

-   Six integrated lashing points for secure load management
-   Heavy-duty rubber mats for easy cleaning
-   Enhanced interior lighting, five times brighter than the standard passenger model
-   58 liters of lockable underfloor storage near the rear door in both 90 and 110 models
-   An additional 155 liters of lockable storage in the rear footwell area of the 110 model

Cargo capacity varies between the two models:

-   Defender 90 Hard Top: 1,355 liters (1.4 cubic meters)
-   Defender 110 Hard Top: 2,059 liters (2.0 cubic meters)

The 110 model can accommodate a Euro pallet, making it suitable for a wide range of commercial applications. Additionally, the 110 variant features rear side doors, providing easy access to smaller items stored towards the front of the cargo area.

## Engine Options and Performance

The Land Rover Defender Commercial offers a range of powerful and efficient engine options, all based on the latest Ingenium 3.0-liter six-cylinder turbodiesel technology. These engines are paired with an eight-speed automatic gearbox and feature all-wheel drive capabilities, providing a perfect blend of performance and practicality for commercial use.

### D200 Engine Specs Land Rover Defender Commercial Price Guide

The Defender 90 Hard Top comes exclusively with the D200 engine option. This entry-level powertrain produces 200hp and 500Nm of torque, delivering smooth and responsive performance. Despite being the base model, the D200 offers impressive acceleration, with a 0-62mph time of 9.8 seconds. The engine’s torquey nature and responsive automatic transmission make it feel livelier than the official figures suggest, providing ample power for most commercial applications.

### D250 and D300 Engine Specs

For those requiring more power, the Defender 110 Hard Top offers two higher-output engine options: the D250 and D300.

The D250 engine generates 249hp and 570Nm of torque, providing a noticeable performance boost over the D200. It accelerates from 0-62mph in 8.4 seconds, offering quicker response and improved towing capabilities.

The top-tier D300 engine produces an impressive 300hp and a massive 650Nm of torque. This powerplant delivers exceptional performance, with a 0-62mph time of just 7.0 seconds. While this level of performance may seem excessive for a commercial vehicle subject to van speed limits, it ensures effortless towing and load-carrying capabilities in challenging conditions.

### Fuel Efficiency Land Rover Defender Commercial Price Guide

All Defender Commercial models feature mild hybrid (MHEV) technology, which helps to improve fuel efficiency and reduce emissions. This system cannot run on electric power alone but provides additional instant muscle when needed.

According to official WLTP (Worldwide Harmonized Light Vehicle Test Procedure) figures, all versions of the Defender Hard Top achieve a claimed fuel economy of 32.2mpg. This consistent figure across different engine options may seem surprising, but it reflects the balance between performance and efficiency achieved by the MHEV system.

CO2 emissions for the Defender Commercial range from 230g/km, which is relatively low for a vehicle of this size and capability. It’s worth noting that actual fuel consumption may vary depending on driving conditions, load, and individual driving style.

The combination of powerful engines, advanced transmission, and mild hybrid technology ensures that the Land Rover Defender Commercial delivers robust performance without compromising on efficiency, making it a versatile choice for businesses requiring a capable and economical commercial vehicle.

## Off-Road Capabilities

The Land Rover Defender Commercial has earned its reputation as a formidable off-road vehicle, capable of tackling the most challenging terrains with ease. Its exceptional off-road capabilities make it an ideal choice for businesses and adventurers who need to reach remote locations or navigate through difficult landscapes.

### Terrain Response System

At the heart of the Defender’s off-road prowess lies its advanced Terrain Response System. This innovative technology allows drivers to optimize the vehicle’s performance for various surface conditions. By simply turning one of the air-conditioning knobs, which doubles as the control for the Terrain Response system, users can select the appropriate driving mode for mud, sand, or rocky surfaces. This user-friendly interface ensures that even less experienced off-road drivers can confidently navigate challenging terrains.

The system works in conjunction with Land Rover’s Intelligent All-Wheel Drive, which continuously varies axle torque based on sensor data from driver inputs and vehicle surroundings. This intelligent driveline system not only enhances traction and on-road dynamics but also contributes to improved fuel economy and lower CO2 emissions without compromising off-road capability.

For those seeking even more advanced off-road performance, Land Rover offers optional upgrades such as the more sophisticated Terrain Response 2 and configurable Terrain Response electronics.

### Wading Depth Land Rover Defender Commercial Price Guide

One of the most impressive features of the Defender Commercial is its exceptional wading capability. The vehicle can ford water up to 900mm deep, a feat that sets it apart from many of its competitors. This impressive wading depth is made possible by the Wade program in the Terrain Response 2 system, which allows drivers to cross flooded roads or tracks with confidence.

To assist drivers in navigating through water, Land Rover has incorporated Wade Sensing technology. This innovative feature takes the stress out of driving through water and adds an element of fun to off-road adventures. When approaching water, it’s advisable to drive slowly at first and then build up momentum. The vehicle’s electronics and carefully positioned components, such as the air intake on the wing, contribute to its impressive wading capabilities.

### Ground Clearance

The Defender Commercial boasts an impressive ground clearance of 291mm, which allows it to easily navigate over obstacles and uneven terrain. This generous clearance ensures a smooth and confident ride even in the most challenging environments.

Both the 90 and 110 Hard Top models are equipped with independent coil-sprung suspension, with advanced Electronic Air Suspension available on the 110 model. The Electronic Air Suspension provides outstanding off-road geometry, with approach, breakover, and departure angles matching those of the passenger models. For the 110 model in Off-Road Height, these angles can reach up to 38, 28, and 40 degrees respectively.

To further enhance its off-road capabilities, the Defender Commercial is equipped with hill descent control and a twin-speed transfer box that can be set to low-range mode. These features, combined with the eight-speed automatic transmission, provide better control over treacherous ground.

## FAQs Land Rover Defender Commercial Price Guide

**1\. What is the starting price for a commercial Land Rover Defender?**  
The base model of the Defender 90 Hard Top is priced from £65,820, which includes on-the-road costs but excludes VAT. For the Defender 110 Hard Top, prices start at £83,012 under the same conditions.

**2\. Does the Land Rover Defender qualify as a commercial vehicle?**  
Since 2011, all classic Defender models have been categorized as “Commercial” vehicles. This classification means they are subject to commercial road tax with the DVLA. Generally, only the hard top and pickup versions of the Defender are recognized as commercial vehicles.

**3\. Is the Land Rover Defender considered a commercial vehicle for tax purposes?**  
The HMRC has clarified that for tax purposes, Land Rover Defenders are treated as cars. This classification applies regardless of the model.

**4\. What are the financing options for a Land Rover Defender?**  
The cost to finance a Land Rover Defender can vary based on several factors including the model, the terms of the finance agreement, and your creditworthiness. It’s advisable to consult with a financial advisor or a dealership to get precise figures and options available.
